Frequently Asked Questions

What are the main advantages of HDPE pipes over traditional materials?

HDPE offers leak-proof fusion joints, excellent chemical resistance, flexibility for trenchless installs, and a design life often exceeding 50 years—reducing maintenance and lifecycle cost for water, gas, and industrial systems.

Which pressure ratings and SDR series do you supply?

We manufacture pipes across common PN classes and SDR profiles (e.g. SDR 11 through SDR 33) to match your project’s pressure, diameter, and installation method. Share your specification and our team will confirm availability.

Can HDPE pipes be used for potable water applications?

Yes—when produced from certified PE100 compounds and tested to relevant standards (such as IS 4984 / ISO 4427), HDPE is widely used for municipal drinking water networks worldwide.

How are joints formed on site?

Butt fusion and electrofusion are the most common methods, creating homogeneous, leak-free joints. Mechanical couplings may be used where appropriate. We can train your crew or recommend certified contractors.

Do you provide coils and custom lengths?

Coils are available for smaller diameters to speed installation; straight lengths are supplied for larger sizes. Custom lengths and project packing can be arranged—contact us with your bill of materials.

Advanced HDPE Pipe Manufacturing Process

Our state-of-the-art extrusion technology ensures consistent quality, optimal material properties, and dimensional accuracy in every pipe we manufacture.

High-Grade Raw Material Selection

We source PE100 virgin resin from approved suppliers and verify every lot for melt flow index, density, and stress-crack resistance before it enters production.

  • PE100 grade material
  • Optimal molecular weight distribution

Precision Single-Screw Extrusion

Resin is melted under controlled temperature profiles and pushed through a die to form a continuous hollow profile with uniform wall thickness.

  • Temperature zones tuned per SDR
  • Screen packs for melt cleanliness
  • Stable output for diameter consistency

Controlled Cooling & Stabilisation

The hot pipe passes through vacuum calibration tanks and spray cooling to lock dimensions and relieve internal stresses gradually.

  • Multi-stage water temperature control
  • Ovality kept within tight tolerances
  • Prevents warping before sizing

Vacuum Sizing & Dimensional Control

Vacuum sizing tanks ensure precise outer diameter while internal pressure maintains perfect roundness and wall thickness uniformity.

  • Laser OD checks in-line
  • Wall thickness monitoring
  • Automatic line speed compensation

Laboratory & In-Line Quality Control

Samples are taken for hydrostatic proof, reversion, and MFI verification. In-line sensors flag any drift before it leaves the line.

  • Batch test records archived
  • Marking verification cameras
  • Non-conforming product isolation

Permanent Product Marking

Each length receives ink-jet or embossed marking with standard reference, SDR, date, and traceability codes for field identification.

  • Standards-compliant legend layout
  • High-contrast durable inks
  • QR links to batch certificate (on request)

Accurate Cutting to Length

Planetary or guillotine cutters deliver square ends ready for fusion, with length tolerances aligned to project cut lists.

  • Coil and stick length programs
  • Chamfer options for easier jointing
  • Bundling tags for site logistics

Protective Packaging & Dispatch

Pipes are end-capped where required, strapped, and stretch-wrapped to prevent movement and UV exposure during transport to your site.

  • Export-ready crating available
  • Loading photos with dispatch docket
  • Partner logistics across India
